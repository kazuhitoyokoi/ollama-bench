" カブトзуキダスト (Cabotusost) is a Japanese onomatopoeic word that refers to the sound made when stepping or walking on dry leaves. This term is often used in children's books, manga, or when describing an action of characters in those media.\n\nIn this context, \"さかなクンとは\" (Sakana Kun to wa) could be translated as \"And what about the fish sound?\" or \"How about the fish's sound?\" This might suggest that the topic is shifting to discuss a related, perhaps humorous, or playful sound made by a fish."

 The phrase "さかなクンとは" is a combination of characters from two Japanese words. "さかな" means "lizard" and "クン" could be a slang term for "yelling," or making noise. So when combined, it refers to a lizard that goes "クン." In English, it can be translated as "The yelling lizard" or "A lizard that goes 'Kun!'".

 サカナクン (Sakanakun) is a Japanese manga series written and illustrated by Shinya Oyari. The manga, which ran from November 2007 to March 2010, tells the story of Takumi Kijima, a high school baseball player struggling with his lack of talent in comparison to his older brother Yojiro, who was an incredible player at the same age. After an encounter with a mysterious creature named Sakanakun, Takumi gains special abilities that help him improve his skills and achieve great heights in the sport. As he develops these newfound powers, Takumi must also find a way to balance them with his everyday life as a student and teammate.

In English, さかなクン (Sakanakun) can be translated to "Baseball's Mysterious Gift" as it involves a baseball-focused story along with supernatural elements in the form of Sakanakun's abilities that help Takumi become a better player.
